--- post_title: 'World’s First Ferroelectric Memristor on a Circuit' author: mlabsadmin post_excerpt: "" layout: post permalink: > /worlds-first-ferroelectric-memristor-on-a-circuit/ --- After the World's simplest memristor i.e. coherer , now its time for the " World's First Ferroelectric Memristor on a Circuit". Panasonic Corporation today announced that it has developed a low energy consumption circuit for neural network systems, by forming the world's first ferroelectric memristor, which can record continuous analog data as resistance, on a CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide Semiconductor) circuit. This is a achieved by them by Crystal growth technique which grows ferroelectric memristor on a CMOS circuit through a seed layer. Here in the atoms in the interlayer insulator film have an unordered arrangement. When a ferroelectric memristor is formed directly on the circuit, the arrangement of the atoms in the ferroelectric film also becomes distorted. Panasonic was successful in designing a ferroelectric memristor with a highly oriented crystal lattice by utilizing a seed layer, which is well ordered. Such chips which imitate human behavior were first known to be possible way back in 1988 through a research work on silicon retina by Caver Mead and Kritof Koch. However, there seems to be a long way to go for such chips. The use of memristor has not only sparked newer interest in these neuromorphic designs but with this work, the future seems much closer.
